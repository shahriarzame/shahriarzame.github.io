## About Me

From analyzing transportation systems in Dhaka to optimizing vertiport networks for urban air mobility in Munich, I have dedicated my career to addressing modern mobility challenges through data-driven insights and innovation. As a graduate of the Transportation Systems program at TUM, I have developed expertise in transportation modeling, agent-based simulations, optimization, GIS, and data analysis. My Master’s thesis (Grade 1.0) focused on optimization, sustainability, and capacity planning for urban air mobility (UAM), honing my proficiency in tools like MATSim, Python, Java, R, SLURM, and QGIS. This work culminated in multiple research papers submitted for academic publishing. My prior experience managing mega infrastructure projects in Bangladesh further strengthened my skills in project management. Looking ahead, I aim to advance research in agent-based modeling, simulation, optimization, artificial intelligence, data analysis, and visualization to shape the future of sustainable transportation systems.
